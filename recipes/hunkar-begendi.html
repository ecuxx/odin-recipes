Hunkar Begendi

Main dish found in the Sultan's palace during the Ottoman Empire. The name means 'The Sultan liked it'. The Sultan's cook was experimenting with new dishes and created this one. When the Sultan enjoyed the dish, cook cheered and said 'SULTAN LIKED IT!!'. He was happy because he saved his head for that night.

Ingredients:

    For begendi:

  • 2 eggplants
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cup shredded cheese

    • For the sauce:

  • 200 grams beef
  • 1 shredded tomato
  • 1 onion
  • 2 green peppers
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon pepper paste
  • 1 cup hot water
  • Salt, black pepper, chili pepper, thyme

Steps:

  1. Roast and peel the eggplants.
  2. In a pan fry the flour in butter and then add the milk. Stir quickly and add the eggplants and cheese. Turn off the heat.
  3. Cook the beef in another pot until it absorbs its water.
  4. Add butter to the beef.
  5. Add peppers, onions and fry them. Then add the tomatoes, spices, pepper paste and hot water, Cook until the sauce thickens.
  6. Pour the sauce over the begendi.
