Hunkar Begendi

Main dish which is found in Sultan's palace in Ottoman Empire. Meaning of name is 'Sultan liked it'. Sultan's cook was trying to do new dishes and created this one. When Sultan liked the dish, cook cheered and said 'SULTAN LIKED IT!!'. Basically he was happy because he saved his head for this night.

Ingredients:

    For begendi:

  • 2 eggplants
  • 1 glass of milk
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 glass of shreded cheese

    • For the sauce:

  • 200 grams beef
  • 1 shreded tomato
  • 1 onion
  • 2 green pepper
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon pepper paste
  • 1 glass hot water
  • Salt, black pepper, chili pepper, thyme

Steps:

  1. Roast and peel the eggplants.
  2. Fry the flour with butter and add milk. Stir quickly and add eggplants and cheese. Turn off the heat.
  3. Cook the beef in another pot until they absorbed their water.
  4. Add butter.
  5. Add peppers, onions and fry them then add the tomatoes, spices, pepper paste and water, Cook them until they are thick.
  6. Pour the sauce on begendi.
